wReach

Let's face it, we don't talk to our neighbours the same way we use to anymore.



Ever since the "stranger danger" campaigns in the 90s, we as a society have become more cautious of who we meet and interact with. Thus followed: friendships through a computer screen. Oh wait how do we do this in person again?

Despite being fortunate enough to have a strong community within my program and the convenience of living in a student town with others my age, it is still difficult to make new friends sometimes, especially if those social circles have been  established as it typically is in post-secondary societies.

So a group of friends and I noted some issues that we were still facing despite being in such a socially rich environment.
‍
Some social "barriers" we were facing:

  • Exploring other interests with someone new - Let's say you're a math student who wants to go to the local tap dancing show. You know you wouldn't want to go alone, you'd rather go with someone. But non of your friends are into tap! Odds are there has to be someone near you who would be interested, right?
  • Arranging casual games - Not every each one of your friends is going to want to play flag football with you this evening, but someone has to!
  • Making new friends - Meeting new people is easy, converting them into friendships is hard. It's awkward to arrange to meet with someone you don't know very well to get to know them better.


The targets?

  • Average Joe. - Just wants to make a friend, grad a drink and explore the city who can tell it from another tale.
  • Shy Individuals - These are the kids who may not be as comfortable going to the larger networking events or feel comfortable complimenting someone's jacket without feeling as if they are inconveniencing them. They want to meet new people, they just don't know how to.
  • Co-op student / Expats - As co-op student in the Waterloo program, we easily find ourselves moving from place to place city to city, having to find the new balance of building your social structure and a whole life for yourself.


Prying yourself into local established social circles is extremely difficult if you don't have an insider or if those circles don't have a well known public presence. Perhaps you want a smaller, more intimate circle? Shouldn't there be an easier way to do this?
‍
What Exists?
We looked at the different ways "meet-up" networking was being handled:

  • Facebook events - Small intimate get-togethers or large public events
  • Tinder - One on one togethers for dating, not for casual connections
  • Bumble - One on one friend get togethers, you match with a person, not necessarily by interests
  • Couchsurfing - Group gatherings for people who are travelling
  • Meet-up - A club based casual meet-up, seem to require lots of commitment and organizational effort
  • Eventbrite - Large scale community events (ticket based)
  • Reddit Subreddits - Typically used for larger get-togethers, also not the platform intended for such use.


Some were aimed for too large and broad, some required a lot of time and investment in community building, others do not suit the type of social interaction one may necessarily be going for. We needed to aim at connections that are at a smaller, stronger scale on a safe platform with trusted stranger and can be an one time get-together or can prosper into something much more.
‍
And that's why we built this thing.

wReach is a platform for students built on the openness to meet new people and to exercise their diversity of interests with new companions.

** Due to Covid-19 we are unable to release our first launch of this platform (don't want people meeting just yet). Perhaps in fall?

Eirene

is a platform that allow users to collectively generate information of the highest priority need of resources in the aftermath of a disaster. These requests are then translated to an interactive and dynamic map, pinning the location of the request and allow humanitarian organizations to analyze the demand for aid using various filters and statistics.

Check us out at : https://devpost.com/software/eirene
