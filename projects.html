Buzz

  For our first semester design course, our design group was challenged with tackling health and wellness for upper year students at the University of Waterloo. After conducting a survey, we found that students were finding a hard time staying awake and paying attention in class.
 To tackle this issue, we approached the project using the iterative design process to ultimately come up with “Buzz”, the shock bracelet. Our final prototype was created by using an arduino set-up with a heart rate sensor and an accelerometer alongside an app prototype created on azure.
 The wristlet monitors the pulse and movement of the user to determine whether or not they are falling asleep. Once fatigue or sleep is detected, the wristlet will buzz accordingly to your custom adjusted setting. The product is controlled through an mobile app connected to your Buzz device via bluetooth.

Snack Overflow


Winner of EngHacks 2018


 The team and I built a IoT liquid monitoring device aimed towards the food industry.
 We wanted to build an app that improves service and profitability. The current industry standard for service is to rely on the visual detection of waitstaff for when drinks need to be refilled. Our app removes signal detection bias allowing for better service and a higher profitability for restaurants.
 Our app allows for a real time visual report of customer’s drink/liquid levels at the various locations of the restaurant. If a drink runs out, waiters can immediately come by to inquire or to refill a drink, saving time and improving profit margins.

Alexa in Waterloo

 As an undergraduate research assistant this term, I have the pleasure of working under Dr.Igor Ivkovic.
 We received from funding from Amazon to seek and investigate the integration of Alexa products into the post-secondary school environments. Whether that’d be helping professors assisting them in replying to their emails, helping students receive their courses from learn or even allowing Alexa to assist with open house events, answering the most commonly asked question: where’s the washroom?
 Throughout this investigation, we will investigate dialogue management as well as natural language processing, eventually publishing a paper to report our findings and research.
 Machine learning will be integrated as a part of finding the best way to process questions aimed towards UWaterloo students and the UWaterloo community.
 Resources will be available as soon as they are published.

BusMe

 Growing up in the GTA made me an extremely frequent commuter. However with a busy school schedule and a long-journey commute, I often found times of rest on the bus or subway. With that comes consequences such as missing your stop for not paying attention or falling asleep.
 Thus, I’ve decided to tackle this problem by developing an app such that as you near your destination bus stop, you will be nudged or notified of your upcoming stop so that you’ll never have to miss your stop again. This app in application could also be helpful for those who are taking public transit in a foreign city for ease of navigation.
 This project is an exercise of UI design and App development using map APIs.
